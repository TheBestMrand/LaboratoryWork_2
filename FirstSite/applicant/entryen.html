    Entry




    Individuals with a complete general secondary education are accepted to study for a bachelor's degree (medical specialist).


    Admission to the first year of study for a bachelor's degree (master's degree in medicine) based on a complete general secondary education is carried out in the form of an external independent assessment, entrance exams or an interview in the cases provided for in the Admission Rules.


    Also, the university accepts for the first (with a shortened period of study) or second (third) course (with a normative period of study for vacant places) persons who have obtained the educational and qualification level of a junior specialist, for a specified number of places to obtain a bachelor's degree (educational and qualification level specialist in the medical direction) on the condition of admission to a related field of training.


    Entrants based on full general secondary education for full-time education, except for persons who have the right to participate in the competition based on the results of entrance exams, enrollment based on interviews and quotas, submit applications only in electronic form online in accordance with the Procedure for submitting and reviewing applications in electronic form for participation in the competitive selection to higher educational institutions.


    All other categories of entrants submit documents in paper form to the admissions committee within the time limits set for accepting documents.


    The entrant may submit copies of documents that are certified by the admissions commission or in accordance with the procedure established by law. However, copies of documents without presenting the originals will not be considered.


    When participating in the competition, the original documents are submitted by the entrant only once (fulfilling the requirements for enrollment). Persons who have not submitted to the admissions committee the originals of documents about the educational (educational and qualification) level and its annex, certificates of external independent evaluation and other documents provided for in the Rules of admission to the university, lose the right to enroll in state-ordered studies, and also for studies at the expense of state preferential long-term loans.
